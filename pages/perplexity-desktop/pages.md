# Pages & Publishing

Perplexity is excellent at researching complex topics, but reading through a long chat thread isn't always the best way to consume information. **Perplexity Pages** solves this by converting your research into a beautifully formatted, published article.

## What are Perplexity Pages?

When you finish a deep dive research thread, you can click "Convert to Page." Perplexity takes all the questions you asked, all the answers it provided, and all the sources it found, and synthesizes them into a single, cohesive document.

> **Expert Tip:** Think of Pages as Wikipedia articles that you generate on demand. You do the messy research in the chat, and Pages creates the polished final product for you to share.

## Key Features

- **Auto-Formatting:** Pages automatically generates a title, table of contents, headers, and bullet points.
- **Visuals:** It automatically searches for and embeds relevant images and charts.
- **Source Preservation:** All the footnote citations from your research are preserved in the final article.
- **Live Embeds:** You can embed live widgets (like stock tickers or weather) directly into the page.

## Step-by-Step: Creating a Page

1. **Research:** Start a thread and ask 3-4 deep questions about a topic.
   - *"What are the economic impacts of universal basic income?"*
   - *"What were the results of the 2024 trials in Finland?"*
   - *"What are the main arguments against it?"*
2. **Convert:** Click the **"Convert to Page"** button at the top right of the thread.
3. **Edit:** The Page editor opens. You can rewrite sections, ask the AI to expand on certain paragraphs, or change the tone (e.g., from "Academic" to "Beginner").
4. **Publish & Share:** Click Publish. You get a public URL that you can share with colleagues, post on social media, or embed in your company wiki.

## Real-World Use Case: Study Guides

**Scenario:** You are taking a university course on European History.

1. Create a Perplexity Collection called "History 101."
2. Ask questions about the French Revolution throughout the week.
3. At the end of the week, click "Convert to Page."
4. Tell the Page editor: *"Format this as a study guide with a summary of key dates, major figures, and a practice quiz at the bottom."*
5. You now have a custom, cited study guide that you can share with your classmates.

## Pro Tips

> **Pro-Tip:** You can ask the Page Editor to rewrite the entire article for a specific audience. Say: *"Rewrite this entire page so that a 5th grader could understand it,"* or *"Rewrite this to sound like a professional investment memo."*

> **Warning:** While Pages are great for formatting, they are not ideal for highly creative writing (like writing a novel or a highly stylized marketing email). For that, you should use the **AI Sandwich** method and take your Perplexity research over to Claude.
