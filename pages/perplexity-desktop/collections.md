# Collections: Organizing Your Brain

**Summary:** Collections allow you to group related search threads into folders and apply specific "Custom Instructions" to all searches within that folder.

**The "Non-Tech" Translation:**
Think of a Collection as a specialized filing cabinet where the librarian wears a different hat for each drawer. In the "Fitness" drawer, the librarian only brings you information from medical journals. In the "Coding" drawer, they only bring you code snippets in Python.

### Context-Aware Recommendations
Claude's "Projects" are designed for uploading huge static files that the AI needs to memorize. Perplexity's "Collections" are designed for organizing your active, real-time web searches and telling the AI *how* to search for that specific topic.

### Steps to Use Collections

1. **Create a Collection:** In the left sidebar of Perplexity, click the "+" next to Collections and give it a name (e.g., "Investment Research").
2. **Set a Custom Prompt:** This is the secret sauce. Add an instruction like: "Always format financial data into a table. Only use sources from the SEC or major financial news outlets."
3. **Start Searching:** Open the Collection and start a new thread. Every search in this folder will automatically obey your custom prompt.
4. **Invite Collaborators:** You can share Collections with teammates so everyone benefits from the same curated research environment.

**The "Aha!" Moment:**
Realizing you never have to type "format this as a table" again because your "Data Analysis" Collection does it automatically for every search.
